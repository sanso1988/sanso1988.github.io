Proximal Policy Optimization

We had talked about vanilla policy gradient algorithm(VPG), which reduces the estimation error via purifying the noise information. Compare to simple policy gradient algorithm(SPG), VPG improves the data efficiency in a theoretical way (find a better estimator).

There's still some things could be done in the optimization process. How big step should take before going to road downhill with current interacting data and estimator. Spinning up explanation is clear.

But even seemingly small differences in parameter space can have very large differences in performance—so a single bad step can collapse the policy performance. This makes it dangerous to use large step sizes with vanilla policy gradients, thus hurting its sample efficiency. [1]

Schulman introduces trust region policy optimization algorithm (TRPO) to deal with this problem[2]. Basic idea is to draw some regions updated parameters shouldn't go out, then takes as biggest step as possible towards the hill. K-L divergence is used to measure the differences or distance between old and new stochastic policy for setting the regions. It turns the problem to a constrained optimization problem. I readed the paper, a lot of mathematic tricks really scare me, but i respect the idea most.

Schulman proposes an alternative algorithm, Proximal Policy Optimization (PPO) seems to perform at least as well as TRPO[1] [3]. What's amazing is understandable, less mathmatical tricks, easy to implement. I just write PPO blog.

PPO gives two approaches, one is penalty, other is clipping. PPO-Penalty leads K-L divergence to the objective to punish huge policy changes, PPO-Clipped makes a clipped surrogate objective to remove the incentives for taking huge step. I just implement the PPO-Clipped.

 

PPO Clipped Surrogate Objective Function

Let rt(θ) denote the policy changes πθ(at|st)πθold(at|st), A^t denotes advantage estimation. The normal objective is

E^[rt(θ)A^t]=E^[πθ(at|st)πθold(at|st)A^t]

It need to clarify πθ(at|st)πθold(at|st)A^t is a proper surrogate objective for Eπθ[G(τ)], detail could be found in [2]. A brief instruction is use approximate distribution to replace with exact one, and remove constant of steps in a trajectory.

What's amazing idea of PPO is to remove the incentives of taking too big step which leads to a big change of policy. The trick is clipping. Let the goal be

E^[min(rt(θ)A^t,clip(rt(θ),1−ϵ,1+ϵ)A^t)]

Figure below[3] shows clipping intuition.

Actually PPO is very similar to VPG, the difference is optimization process (take many or big steps ) and objective. Normally, ϵ set to 0.02 [3].
